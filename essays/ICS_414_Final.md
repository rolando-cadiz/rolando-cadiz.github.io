## Introduction
My experience in ICS 414 was one of the most practical and valuable parts of my computer science education so far. Unlike many programming assignments that focus on isolated concepts, ICS 414 gave me the opportunity to work on a larger software project with a team. Because of this experience, I gained a much better understanding of what it means to build software beyond simply writing code.

## The Group Project
For our group project, we worked on Shelf Awareness, a pantry inventory management system designed to help users keep track of their ingredients, manage recipes, and make better use of the food they already have. My main contributions included the user interface overhaul and rebrand, the recipe rating feature, and the recipe sorting feature. I also helped with debugging and fixing issues throughout the development process. Working on a team project made the experience feel much closer to real software development because our individual work had to fit into a larger shared application.

## Technical Growth
One of the most meaningful parts of the project for me was building the recipe rating system. This feature made the application feel more complete and interactive because users could provide feedback on recipes instead of simply viewing them. Implementing it helped me understand how the frontend, API routes, database, and user interface all work together. I had to think through how a user submits a rating, how that data is processed, how it is stored, and how the updated rating information is displayed back to the user. This was the point where the project felt less like a class assignment and more like a real software product.

## Challenges and Frustrations
The biggest challenge I faced during ICS 414 was time management. I had a personal goal of closing two to four issues per week, but I also had other classes and a job to balance. Staying on track required me to manage my time carefully and stay consistent even when other responsibilities competed for my attention. Another challenge was learning how APIs work and understanding how the database, backend, and frontend communicate with each other. At first, these parts felt separate, but over time I began to understand how they work together as one connected system.

## Self-Reflection
Working with teammates was also an important part of the experience. There were times when the app did not work as expected, and solving those problems required communication, patience, and collaboration. I learned that software engineering is not only about individual technical ability. It also requires being able to explain problems clearly, read unfamiliar code, ask for help when necessary, and work with others to figure out why something is not working.

## Conclusion
Overall, ICS 414 helped me grow as both a developer and a teammate. I learned a lot about full-stack development, project organization, debugging, and collaborative software engineering. More importantly, the class gave me a more realistic understanding of what building software is like in practice. Through Shelf Awareness, I gained confidence in my ability to contribute to a team project, learn unfamiliar technologies, and work through problems until a feature becomes functional.
